U.S. Nuclear Regulatory System - IDIQ Contract: A Showcase of KMC's Expertise and Agility

Project Overview: KMC's engagement with the U.S. Nuclear Regulatory System under a multi-year IDIQ contract exemplifies our capacity to provide extensive and expert IT support. This project underscores our ability to implement large-scale, architected solutions alongside agile, quick-turnaround developments.

Core Deliverables:

  • Skilled Personnel: Deployment of highly skilled professionals proficient in a variety of IT domains, including data systems, networks, A/V systems, and voice technologies. This reflects KMC's commitment to offering top-tier talent for complex IT challenges.
  • Specialized IT Support for Emergency Operations Center (EOC): Provision of expert IT services and products specifically tailored for the NRC Headquarters Emergency Operations Center. This demonstrates our capability to cater to specialized IT needs with precision and expertise.
  • Timely and Efficient IT Services: Our approach ensures that IT support for the EOC is not only expert but also delivered in a timely and efficient manner. This highlights KMC's agility and responsiveness in meeting critical timelines.
  • Broad Scope of IT Support: KMC's involvement spans a wide range of IT support activities, including both planned and unplanned tasks, maintenance, development, analysis, and study. This diversity showcases our versatility in handling various IT demands.
  • Comprehensive IT Support Management: All IT support requests from NRC for the EOC are meticulously managed as separate tasks. This level of organization and attention to detail speaks to KMC's thoroughness in project management and client service.

Impact and Value: Through this IDIQ contract, KMC has not only demonstrated its extensive IT capabilities but also its commitment to delivering customized, responsive solutions. This project serves as a testament to KMC's ability to handle large-scale IT projects with agility and precision, reinforcing our reputation as a leader in innovative IT solutions.

U.S. Nuclear Regulatory System - NSIR

Project Overview: KMC's engagement with the U.S. Nuclear Regulatory System - NSIR involves the comprehensive development, testing, training, and support of a document management system. This system is specifically tailored for managing submissions from nuclear power plants nationwide, indicating KMC's capability in handling sensitive and large-scale information management projects.

System Capabilities:

  • Nationwide Document Management: The system is equipped to handle the storage, categorization, uploading, indexing, searching, and retrieval of documents, supporting both the headquarters and regional offices. This ensures a streamlined and efficient document management process across all locations.
  • Replication and Redundancy: A key feature of the system is the automatic replication of documents between the headquarters and regions. Additionally, the system is designed for high availability; if any headquarters or regional server goes offline, the system allows reconfiguration to enable another server to function as the primary server. This redundancy ensures uninterrupted access and reliability.
  • Content Management for Internal Intranet: KMC also played a pivotal role in architecting, developing, and supporting a system to manage content for the internal NRC HQ intranet. This includes vital organizational information such as announcements and organizational charts, highlighting KMC's versatility in handling both document management and internal communication systems.

Project Management and Methodology: KMC has applied its standard process for system architectural efforts, tailored to align with the goals of the NRC / OMB architectural approach. The project utilized Earned Value Management (EVM) techniques and reports, allowing KMC to maintain tight control over contract schedules, budgets, and performance. This meticulous approach to project management ensures not only up-to-date progress tracking but also predictive management of eventual costs and schedules.

Conclusion: The involvement in the U.S. Nuclear Regulatory System - NSIR project underscores KMC's expertise in developing comprehensive IT solutions that cater to the specific needs of government agencies. The success of this project demonstrates KMC's ability to manage complex information systems with precision and reliability, reinforcing their position as a leader in the field of IT services.

National Association of Security Dealers (NASD)

Project Description: KMC played a pivotal role in developing the CRD system for the National Association of Security Dealers (NASD). This system allows both organizations and individual brokers to access NASD Regulation's CRD database information through a web browser.

Technical Challenges and Solutions: Given its use by the majority of NASD brokerages, the system's architecture needed to be robust enough to support high volumes of transactions and numerous concurrent users. KMC's architectural expertise was instrumental in designing a system that not only meets these high demands but also ensures reliability and efficiency in a distributed application environment.

Watson Wyatt Worldwide - SHEETS Project

Project Scope: For Watson Wyatt Worldwide, an international premier human resources consulting firm, KMC undertook a challenging two-month project named SHEETS. The goal was to transition a LAN/WAN-based budget system to a web/internet platform.

Execution and Timeliness: Starting in December, KMC conducted an in-depth analysis and development process with a strict deadline of March 1st. This target date was crucial to align with Watson Wyatt's budget creation window. The project's successful completion within this tight timeframe highlighted KMC's ability to deliver high-quality solutions under pressure.
